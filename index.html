Welcome to PSDP Project (NCHM)

The National Centre for Herbal Medicine (NCHM) is the project dedicated to the research, development, and promotion of herbal medicine and natural remedies. Its primary focus is to study the medicinal properties of plants and develop standardized methods for their effective use in healthcare.

About Us

Pakistan Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (PCSIR) was established in 1953 under Societies Act to promote the cause of Science and Technology in the country. Since 1973, it is functioning under the Act of Parliament, which was amended in 1984. Chief Executive of the Council is the Chairman who is appointed by the Federal Government.

There are eleven Laboratories / Units and five HRD Centres established throughout the country, headed by Director Generals / Directors who directly report to the Chairman. In Head Office 150 officers / staff including 07 Directors are working in different divisions / wings. There are 570 Scientists / Engineers / Technologists working in different Laboratories supported by 859 technicians/skilled workers/supporting staff and 1125 non-technical (administrative/ accounts / security etc.) staff.

Our Core Objectives

Promote Research and Innovation in Herbal Medicine

The NCHM aims to advance scientific research in herbal medicine by exploring the medicinal properties of various plants and developing innovative methods for their application in healthcare. This includes conducting clinical trials, formulating new herbal products, and improving the efficacy of traditional remedies.

Standardize Herbal Medicine Practices

The NCHM strives to create standardized guidelines and protocols for the preparation and use of herbal medicines, ensuring their safety, quality, and effectiveness. This objective aims to bridge the gap between traditional knowledge and modern scientific practices to make herbal treatments more reliable and widely accepted.

Raise Public Awareness and Education

The NCHM works to increase public awareness about the benefits of herbal medicine through educational programs, workshops, and outreach activities. This objective focuses on promoting informed decisions regarding natural health treatments, as well as encouraging sustainable use of herbal resources.
